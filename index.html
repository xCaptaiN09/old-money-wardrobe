—Fabric Guide by Season

Old money is as much about texture as colour. The right fabric makes the outfit — the wrong one breaks it regardless of how good the colours are.

Summer · Hot Weather
Linen
LinenLinen-CottonSeersuckerGauze
The primary old money summer fabric. Wrinkles are acceptable — intentional even. Never iron linen flat. Loose weave, breathable, drops beautifully over Gurkha silhouettes.
Spring · Mild Weather
Cotton & Oxford
Oxford ClothPoplinChino TwillCotton Canvas
Structured but breathable. Oxford cloth shirts are the backbone of the smart casual old money wardrobe. Chino twill for trousers gives shape without heaviness.
Autumn · Transitional
Flannel & Corduroy
FlannelCorduroyMoleskinHeavy Cotton
Flannel trousers are one of the most old money pieces in existence. Corduroy reads heritage and country. Both carry texture that photographs and ages well.
Winter · Cold Weather
Wool & Tweed
TweedMerino WoolLambswoolCashmereHerringbone
Tweed is the single most heritage-coded fabric in old money menswear. Merino and lambswool for knitwear only — no synthetic blends. Cashmere for overcoats and scarves.
All Season · Avoid
Never Use
PolyesterNylonRayonSpandexAcrylic
Synthetic fabrics don't drape, don't breathe, and don't age well. They look cheap under light and feel wrong against natural fabrics. None of these belong in this wardrobe regardless of colour.
Footwear & Accessories
Leather & Suede
Full-grain LeatherSuedeCalfskinCanvas (casual)
Shoes and belts must be full-grain leather or suede — no bonded leather, no synthetic uppers. Suede reads more casual and heritage. Polished leather reads more formal. Both work across this wardrobe.

VIIColours to Avoid

These tones break the natural, muted logic the entire palette is built on.

Black
Too stark, zero warmth
Bright White
Use cream or ivory
Bright Blue
Use navy only
Red
No natural grounding
Purple
No heritage association
Bright Orange
Use terracotta instead
Neon / Lime
Antithetical to every principle
Bright Green
Keep greens dark and muted
Pink / Blush
No heritage grounding
Steel Blue / Teal
Too modern and corporate
Silver / Light Grey
Use warm grey instead
Bright Yellow
Use muted mustard only
